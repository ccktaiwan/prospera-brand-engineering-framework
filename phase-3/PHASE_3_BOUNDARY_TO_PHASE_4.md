PURPOSE

This document defines the canonical boundary conditions
that must be satisfied before any organization
may progress from Phase 3 (Experience & Evidence)
to Phase 4 (Brand Signal & Platform Execution).

This boundary exists to prevent premature amplification
of organizations whose delivery capability
has not yet been externally verifiable.

The purpose of this document is risk containment,
not performance optimization.

==================================================

WHY THIS BOUNDARY EXISTS

In B2B environments, brand amplification
multiplies both credibility and failure.

When amplification precedes evidence:

- Promises outpace delivery
- Trust is transferred implicitly to partners
- Failure costs are externalized
- Brand damage compounds rapidly

Phase 3 exists to establish credibility.
Phase 4 exists to amplify credibility.

This boundary ensures that amplification
does not occur in the absence of evidence.

==================================================

NON-NEGOTIABLE PRINCIPLE

No brand signal amplification,
platform execution,
or growth-oriented communication
may begin unless Phase 3
has been formally completed.

Completion is determined by evidence,
not intention.

==================================================

PHASE 3 COMPLETION REQUIREMENTS

Progression to Phase 4 is permitted
only when all of the following conditions
are simultaneously satisfied.

--------------------------------------------------

REQUIREMENT 1
Comparable Experience Evidence Exists

The organization has documented
completed work demonstrating
comparable levels of complexity,
risk, and responsibility.

Experience must be delivered,
not anticipated.

--------------------------------------------------

REQUIREMENT 2
Execution Paths Are Explainable

The organization can describe
how outcomes were achieved,
including decision points,
constraints, and corrective actions.

Narrative success without execution clarity
does not qualify.

--------------------------------------------------

REQUIREMENT 3
Time and Complexity Are Acknowledged

The organization demonstrates
realistic understanding of
delivery timelines,
dependencies,
and non-linear effort.

Minimization of complexity
invalidates evidence credibility.

--------------------------------------------------

REQUIREMENT 4
External Referenceability Is Possible

Third parties can confirm
how the organization operates,
without reliance on personal advocacy
or founder mediation.

Referenceability does not require endorsement,
only verifiability.

--------------------------------------------------

REQUIREMENT 5
Capability Boundaries Are Declared

The organization explicitly states:

- What work it will not undertake
- Conditions under which it will decline engagement
- Scenarios where capability degrades

Undefined boundaries constitute unmanaged risk.

==================================================

DISQUALIFYING CONDITIONS

The following conditions explicitly prohibit
progression to Phase 4:

- Experience exists only as narrative description
- All credibility depends on founder reputation
- Past work cannot be externally verified
- Complexity is consistently underestimated
- The organization claims universal capability

Any single disqualifying condition
blocks Phase progression.

==================================================

RELATIONSHIP TO PHASE 4

Phase 4 activities include, but are not limited to:

- Brand signal amplification
- Platform presence expansion
- Public positioning
- Partnership scaling
- Market visibility initiatives

These activities multiply perception.

Without Phase 3 completion,
they multiply risk.

==================================================

CANONICAL STATEMENT

Amplification without evidence
does not create growth.

It creates exposure.

Phase 3 exists to ensure
that when an organization is seen,
it is seen for what it can actually deliver.
