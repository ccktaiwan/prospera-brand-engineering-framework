Prospera Brand Engineering Framework
Phase 6 – Scaling & Governance
Canonical Codex
────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 1. PHASE DEFINITION

Phase 6 is the phase in which an enterprise determines whether a governed execution
model may be replicated or institutionalized without increasing governance cost,
decision latency, or risk exposure.

Phase 6 does not represent growth.
Phase 6 represents governable scalability.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 2. PHASE ENTRY CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────

Phase 6 SHALL exist if and only if ALL entry conditions below are satisfied.

+-------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------+
| Entry Condition   | Requirement                                                 |
+-------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------+
| ENTRY_CONDITION_1 | Phase 5 execution reached terminal state: COMPLETED         |
| ENTRY_CONDITION_2 | No unresolved Phase 5 governance violations                 |
| ENTRY_CONDITION_3 | Outcomes are repeatable without individual dependency       |
| ENTRY_CONDITION_4 | Governance oversight cost did not increase in Phase 5       |
+-------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------+

Failure to meet ANY entry condition SHALL block Phase 6 initiation.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3. PHASE PURPOSE (ENTERPRISE FUNCTION)
────────────────────────────────────────

The sole enterprise function of Phase 6 is to verify whether a collaboration model
remains governable when replicated.

Scaling that introduces additional supervision, exception handling, or human
dependency SHALL be considered non-canonical.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 4. PERMITTED ACTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────

+--------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------+
| Action ID          | Permitted Action                                            |
+--------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------+
| PERMITTED_ACTION_1 | Replicate execution using identical governance controls     |
| PERMITTED_ACTION_2 | Institutionalize as standard operating capability           |
| PERMITTED_ACTION_3 | License reuse without governance modification               |
| PERMITTED_ACTION_4 | Formalize ownership without adding oversight layers         |
+--------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------+

All permitted actions MUST preserve Phase 5 execution constraints.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5. PROHIBITED ACTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────

+---------------------+------------------------------------------------------------+
| Action ID           | Prohibited Action                                          |
+---------------------+------------------------------------------------------------+
| PROHIBITED_ACTION_1 | Scaling requiring exceptional management attention         |
| PROHIBITED_ACTION_2 | Substituting trust or success for governance control        |
| PROHIBITED_ACTION_3 | Replicating partner-specific accommodations                |
| PROHIBITED_ACTION_4 | Expanding scope without governance architecture redesign   |
+---------------------+------------------------------------------------------------+

Any prohibited action constitutes non-canonical scaling.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 6. INTERNAL GOVERNANCE STATE MODEL
────────────────────────────────────────

+------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+
| Governance State       | Definition                                                |
+------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+
| EVALUATION             | Governance fitness assessment                             |
| REPLICATION            | Controlled replication under identical rules              |
| INSTITUTIONALIZATION   | Embedded as standard enterprise capability                |
| GOVERNED_EXPANSION     | Limited expansion under unchanged governance              |
| SCALING_TERMINATED     | Scaling permanently halted                                |
+------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+

Scaling MAY occur ONLY in REPLICATION and GOVERNED_EXPANSION.
SCALING_TERMINATED is a terminal governance state.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 7. ENTERPRISE DECISION INTERPRETATION MATRIX
────────────────────────────────────────

+-------------------------------+-------------------+----------------------+----------------------+
| Observed Scaling Behavior     | Legal             | Procurement          | Management           |
+-------------------------------+-------------------+----------------------+----------------------+
| Identical-governance replica  | Defensible        | Cost predictable     | Scalable             |
| Additional oversight needed   | Risk escalation   | Cost uncertainty     | Reject scaling       |
| Individual dependency exists  | Non-transferable  | Vendor lock-in risk  | Reject scaling       |
| Exception frequency increases | Governance failure| Cost leakage         | Terminate scaling    |
+-------------------------------+-------------------+----------------------+----------------------+

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 8. COMPLIANCE AND VIOLATION CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────

+-----------------------------+-------------------------+-----------------------------+
| Violation Type              | Classification          | Enterprise Action           |
+-----------------------------+-------------------------+-----------------------------+
| Non-repeatable scaling      | Non-canonical scaling   | Suspend scaling             |
| Increased governance cost   | Governance inflation    | Redesign or rollback        |
| Partner-dependent scale     | Structural risk         | Terminate Phase 6           |
| Exception-driven expansion  | Governance breach       | Revert to Phase 5           |
+-----------------------------+-------------------------+-----------------------------+

Violations SHALL invalidate Phase 6 eligibility.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 9. CANONICAL CLOSURE STATEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────

Phase 6 is not the phase where success is amplified.
It is the phase where governance survives replication.

Only models that remain governable when scaled
are eligible for long-term enterprise adoption.
