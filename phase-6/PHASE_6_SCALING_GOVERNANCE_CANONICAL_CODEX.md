Phase 6 – Scaling & Governance
Canonical Codex
────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 1. PHASE DEFINITION

Phase 6 is the phase in which an enterprise evaluates whether a governed execution model
may be replicated, extended, licensed, or institutionalized
without increasing governance cost or risk exposure.

Phase 6 does not represent growth.
Phase 6 represents governable scalability.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 2. PHASE ENTRY CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────

Phase 6 SHALL exist if and only if ALL of the following conditions are met:

ENTRY_CONDITION_1: Phase 5 execution has reached a terminal state identified as COMPLETED.
ENTRY_CONDITION_2: No unresolved Phase 5 governance violation exists.
ENTRY_CONDITION_3: Execution outcomes demonstrate repeatability under governance.
ENTRY_CONDITION_4: Enterprise governance cost did not increase during Phase 5 execution.

If ANY entry condition is not satisfied, Phase 6 SHALL NOT be initiated.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3. PHASE PURPOSE (ENTERPRISE FUNCTION)
────────────────────────────────────────

The sole enterprise function of Phase 6 is:

To determine whether collaboration outcomes can be expanded or replicated
without introducing additional governance complexity.

Any scaling action that increases governance burden
violates Phase 6 principles.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 4. PERMITTED ACTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────

During Phase 6, the enterprise MAY perform ONLY the following actions:

PERMITTED_ACTION_1: Replicate the execution model across business units or regions.
PERMITTED_ACTION_2: License or authorize controlled reuse of the execution model.
PERMITTED_ACTION_3: Institutionalize the collaboration as a standard enterprise capability.
PERMITTED_ACTION_4: Define or formalize long-term governance structures.

ALL permitted actions MUST preserve Phase 5 governance constraints.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5. PROHIBITED ACTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────

During Phase 6, the enterprise SHALL NOT perform ANY of the following actions:

PROHIBITED_ACTION_1: Scale execution models that failed governance controls.
PROHIBITED_ACTION_2: Substitute scale for governance discipline.
PROHIBITED_ACTION_3: Assume trust replaces control.
PROHIBITED_ACTION_4: Replicate partner-specific exceptions.
PROHIBITED_ACTION_5: Expand scope without governance redesign.

Any prohibited action constitutes non-canonical scaling.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 6. INTERNAL GOVERNANCE MODEL
────────────────────────────────────────

Phase 6 operates under the following governance states:

GOVERNANCE_STATE_1: EVALUATION
GOVERNANCE_STATE_2: REPLICATION
GOVERNANCE_STATE_3: INSTITUTIONALIZATION
GOVERNANCE_STATE_4: GOVERNED_EXPANSION
GOVERNANCE_STATE_5: SCALING_TERMINATED

SCALING MAY occur ONLY in the following states:

SCALING_ALLOWED_STATE_1: REPLICATION
SCALING_ALLOWED_STATE_2: GOVERNED_EXPANSION

SCALING_TERMINATED is a terminal governance state.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 7. ENTERPRISE INTERPRETATION RULES (DECISION TABLE)
────────────────────────────────────────

OBSERVED_BEHAVIOR_1: Replicable with identical controls
LEGAL_INTERPRETATION_1: Defensible
PROCUREMENT_INTERPRETATION_1: Cost-predictable
MANAGEMENT_INTERPRETATION_1: Scalable

OBSERVED_BEHAVIOR_2: Requires additional oversight
LEGAL_INTERPRETATION_2: Risk escalation
PROCUREMENT_INTERPRETATION_2: Cost uncertainty
MANAGEMENT_INTERPRETATION_2: Scaling hesitation

OBSERVED_BEHAVIOR_3: Depends on individual partner
LEGAL_INTERPRETATION_3: Non-transferable
PROCUREMENT_INTERPRETATION_3: Vendor lock-in risk
MANAGEMENT_INTERPRETATION_3: Reject scaling

OBSERVED_BEHAVIOR_4: Increases exception handling
LEGAL_INTERPRETATION_4: Governance failure
PROCUREMENT_INTERPRETATION_4: Cost leakage
MANAGEMENT_INTERPRETATION_4: Terminate scaling

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 8. COMPLIANCE AND VIOLATION CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────

VIOLATION_TYPE_1: Scaling without repeatability
GOVERNANCE_CLASSIFICATION_1: Non-canonical scaling
ENTERPRISE_ACTION_1: Scaling suspension

VIOLATION_TYPE_2: Increased governance cost
GOVERNANCE_CLASSIFICATION_2: Governance inflation
ENTERPRISE_ACTION_2: Governance redesign required

VIOLATION_TYPE_3: Partner-dependent scaling
GOVERNANCE_CLASSIFICATION_3: Structural risk
ENTERPRISE_ACTION_3: Scaling termination

VIOLATION_TYPE_4: Exception-driven expansion
GOVERNANCE_CLASSIFICATION_4: Governance breach
ENTERPRISE_ACTION_4: Revert to Phase 5 execution

Violations SHALL invalidate Phase 6 eligibility.

────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 9. CANONICAL CLOSURE STATEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────

Phase 6 is not the phase where success is amplified.
It is the phase where governance survives scale.

Only collaboration models that remain governable when replicated
are eligible for long-term institutional adoption.
